Mauritius: best places for romance

Mauritius is the perfect love island. Whether you want to walk along a secluded beach, take a catamaran to a secluded cove, or just watch the world go by from the veranda of your hotel, it’s all there waiting for you

Published: 11:16 BST Friday, 12 June 2015

Brought to you by:

About this content

As a popular wedding and honeymoon destination, Mauritius is made for romance. Magic moments that could be yours include sipping rum sundowners on a stunning white beach, hopping aboard a catamaran for a trip to a pristine offshore island or taking a walk in one of the island’s lush natural parks. Read on for ideas for more romantic ideas – without the cheese.

1 Hidden beaches
The island’s most scenic drive takes you from lush Bel Ombre to Le Morne peninsula, through sleepy Creole fishing villages, deserted beaches and forests. At journey’s end you will find a handful of exclusive hotels that border the 4km white-sand Le Morne beach, one of the island’s finest with the dramatic Le Morne mountain as its backdrop. Tranquil and private, it’s the perfect beach to walk hand in hand on. No buses reach this isolated outpost and there are few cars, making it the ideal spot for a bike ride à deux. If you’re feeling active, go horse riding with the island’s most exclusive stables.

2 Nature lovers
One of the island’s easiest and prettiest nature walks is at La Vallée de Ferney, a 200-hectare reserve in the Bambous mountains. Meander along a 3km path through the Ferney forest, making sure that you look out for the endangered Mauritius kestrel. Be sure to stop at the Fearney Lodge, a restaurant nestled among endemic ebony trees serving traditional Mauritian cuisine. For a walk on the island’s wild side, take the clifftop path from Gris Gris viewpoint to a promontory to watch the waves wash over La Roche qui Pleure (the rock that weeps).

3 Spectacular views
It’s easy to see why Cap Malheureux, at the island’s most northerly tip, is one of Mauritius’ most photographed sites. From the pretty red-roofed Roman Catholic church of Notre Dame Auxiliatrice by the sea, you can experience spectacular views over the Northern Islands. Near the village of Poudre d’Or is the monument to Le Saint Géran, the shipwreck that inspired the romantic novel Voyage à l’Ile de France by French writer Jacques-Henri Bernardin de Saint-Pierre, about star-crossed lovers Paul and Virginie.

4 Romantic restaurants
For candle-lit fine dining à deux, it’s hard to beat the veranda of the elegant
19th-century colonial mansion Le Château de Bel Ombre which serves Mauritian-fusion cuisine using locally-sourced ingredients. Romantic hideaway La Maison d’Été serves good-value elegant French-style lunches in its open-sided thatched restaurant on the rocky, windswept north-east shore. Blow the budget on a heli-lunch taking in a breathtaking bird’s eye view of the island before dining at one of the island’s top restaurants, such as Varangue sur Morne in the Chamarel highlands, offering a wild-game menu and sweeping views over Le Morne.

5 Sail away
Part of the romance of visiting Mauritius’ offshore islands is sunbathing on the deck of a catamaran with cocktail in hand. Choose from popular, crescent-shaped Ile aux Bénitiers off the west coast, with golden sand and good snorkelling at Crystal Rock, or take an exclusive day trip to Île Plate (Flat Island), the largest of the Northern Islands with lovely beaches and an upmarket restaurant, the Governor’s House, set in a romantic 19th-century ruin. Most romantic of all is being met with perfumed towels at the jetty and served sparkling wine in a billowing white tent on an all-inclusive day trip to private island Île des Deux Cocos (Island of the Two Coconuts), fringed with blinding white sand, with snorkeling in spectacular Blue Bay Marine Park, a five-star gourmet lunch and rhum arrangés to while away the afternoon.

British Airways operates three flights a week to Mauritius from London Gatwick.
