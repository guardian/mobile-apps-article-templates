A panel of Guardian reporters explores the fallout from the Panama Papers and tells the inside story behind the biggest leak in history

The Panama Papers have revealed the myriad ways the rich can exploit secretive offshore tax regimes, and had repercussions across the globe. The release of the files led to the dramatic departure of the Icelandic prime minister, Sigmundur Davíð Gunnlaugsson, and put David Cameron on the political precipice after several bungled attempts to explain his own connections with offshore schemes.

Will Cameron be able to recover from the most difficult week of his premiership? What are the implications of the $2bn offshore trail that leads to Vladimir Putin? Is the Mossack Fonseca leak just the tip of the iceberg – and what happens next?

A panel of Guardian journalists – deputy editor Paul Johnson, head of investigations Nick Hopkins, reporter David Pegg, investigations correspondent Holly Watt, business reporter Juliette Garside and foreign correspondent Luke Harding – came together at a Guardian Live event to discuss the issue.
